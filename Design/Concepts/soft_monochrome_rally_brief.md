# Soft Monochrome Rally

## Visual Core

- The app should feel like a calm riding instrument, not like a taxi app, fitness app, or generic maps tool.
- The interface is map-first, with detached floating elements and generous side breathing room.
- Motorcycle references are abstract: route geometry, compact dashboard-like metrics, precision, and material warmth.

## Color Rules

- Background: `#F3F1EE`
- Surface: `#FBFAF7`
- Track / dividers: `#E9E2D9`
- Primary text: `#1E1E1A`
- Secondary text: `#6F6C66`
- Accent: `#C58A4A`

## Component Rules

- The planner uses a detached outer shell with larger radius than the inner cards.
- Inner controls are grouped into small cards instead of long stacked rows with equal visual weight.
- Buttons and overlays should look like floating capsules or compact rounded blocks.
- The primary CTA uses graphite fill on light screens. Copper is used to emphasize route identity, selected states, and key progress.

## Planner Sheet Rules

- Never let the sheet become a full-width slab from edge to edge.
- Keep a visible map crown above the sheet, even in the large state.
- Separate action dock from planner content.
- Use 16-24pt horizontal gutters to preserve air.
- Limit each planner state to one dominant purpose:
  - Collapsed: summary
  - Medium: planning
  - Large: decision / confirmation

## Motorcycle Cues

- Apex-like route curves
- Tight but readable metrics
- Warm copper as a subtle mechanical / sunset note
- Compact, confident controls instead of playful or bubbly forms

## Things To Avoid

- Pure black and pure white
- Blue accent mixed with copper accent
- Dense settings slabs
- Literal biker illustrations or decorative motorcycle icons in core flows
- Overdone glassmorphism or futuristic HUD styling
